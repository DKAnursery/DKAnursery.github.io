Vegetables

We are working to expand our selection to include a number of favorite vegetables, including our personal favorite, the tomato. We spent the 2022 growing season learning the ropes of starting vegetables from seed and growing them to harvest. We are planning to have a much broader selection of transplants available to you for 2023. This year we are growing more varieties of our favorite vegetables, and trying out some vegetables we haven't grown before to gain experience and get an even better feel for vegetable gardening. If you are interested in transplants or produce from DKA Nursery, please reach out to us or see our availability page! Our contact information can be found on the request page. Happy gardening!

Tomatoes

In 2021 we got hooked on vegetable gardening when we grew seven black cherry tomato plants here at the nursery. We tried several new varieties in 2022, and, and this year we are planning to grow roughly a dozen different tomato varieties. With so many uses, colors, and varieties, tomatoes are our all-around favorite, and we're sure there's one to fit your needs.

Peppers

We grew sweet peppers for the first time in 2022, and they were a smashing success! For 2023, we have expanded our selection to include last year's sweet, cayenne-shaped, Jimmy Nardello along with several bell-pepper varieties. Peppers are extremely productive; they also freeze quite well, allowing you to preserve that abundant harvest.

Cucumbers

Next to tomatoes, Cucumbers are a close second favorite for us. Whether you choose to eat them fresh or make pickles, cucumbers are a delicious vegetable. We grew Cucumbers for the first time in 2022, and they were the best we've ever tasted! With so many varieties specialized for different uses, we are sure there is a cucumber for you.

Beans

Beans come in many variations for many varied uses. We are primarily growing green-bean, also known as snap-bean, varieties, but there are also many great dry-beans out there. We found a little-known seed source of Appalachian Heirloom beans and can't wait to try out several of their highly acclaimed varieties in 2023.

Lettuce

Lettuce is just one of the cool-season vegetables we are growing this year at DKA Nursery. We especially like to use lettuce in salads, and we are trying out a head-lettuce and two leaf-lettuce varieties this year. Lettuces come in many different flavors and colors, so we are sure there is a variety to meet your culinary needs.

Onions

Often overlooked, onions are root vegetables that deserve a better reputation. We are growing onions this year particularly for storage, but they are also great used fresh as a seasoning in a number of dishes as varied as soups and tacos. Onions generally come as white, red, or yellow, and there are various sizes.

Carrots

Carrots are one of the most delicious root crops commonly grown by gardeners. Rich in a number of vitamins, carrots are excellent grated into a salad or enjoyed fresh. The tricky part about root vegetables is determining when to dig them up, but carrots are great eating at nearly any stage, so this isn't as much of a problem.

Radishes

Radishes are perhaps the most quickly maturing of any vegetable we know of. Some varieties are ready for harvest in less than 30 days for sowing! There are also longer-maturing types, called winter radishes. This year, we are sticking with the quick-growing spring radishes. These are great for interplanting, and add a nice zest to salads.

Garlic

Garlic is probably the most pest-free plant in any garden. This allium family bulb is related to onions and leeks. We grow garlic at our rural location because it is the only vegetable we grow not bothered by deer. Homegrown garlic is so much more flavorful than the store-bought kind, even after being stored most of the winter.
