Tomatoes

In 2021 we got hooked on vegetable gardening when we grew seven black cherry tomato plants here at the nursery. We tried several new varieties in 2022, and this year we are planning to grow roughly a dozen total heirloom varieties. With so many uses, colors, and varieties, tomatoes are our all-around favorite, and we're sure there's one to fit your needs.

Below you will find 11 varieties of tomatoes. We are planning to try out nearly all of them in 2023, so let us know if you are interested in any of these outstanding varieties! If you have any questions or comments, please let us know! Our contact information can be found on our Request Page. Thank you for your interest!

Arkansas Marvel

Yellow-Orange bicolor, indeterminate, beefsteak variety. The ultimate BLT tomato.

Black Krim

Dark Purple/Black, indeterminate, beefsteak variety. Good for sandwiches or cooking.

Black Ethiopian

Dark Purple/Black, indeterminate, medium-sized variety. Good for fresh eating.

Indian River

Red, indeterminate, medium-sized variety. Good for early season eating or canning.

Cream Sausage

Yellow, determinate, sausage-shaped variety. Good for fresh eating or for sauce.

Beliy Naliv

Red, determinate, medium-sized tomato variety. Early harvest, good for fresh eating.

Chiapas Wild

Red, indeterminate, tiny cherry variety. Good in salads, also a good keeper.

Amish Paste

Red, indeterminate, giant paste tomato variety. Good for making tomato sauce.

Watermelon Beefsteak

Light Red/Pink, indeterminate, WV heirloom beefsteak variety. Giant slicing/sandwich tomatoes.

Black Cherry

Dark Purple/Black, indeterminate, cherry variety. Prone to splitting. Good eaten fresh in salads.

Chapman

Red, indeterminate, beefsteak variety. Good on sandwiches or for making sauce.
