Garlic

Garlic is probably the most pest-free plant in any garden. This allium family bulb is related to onions and leeks. We grow garlic off-site at our rural location because it is the only vegetable we grow not bothered by deer. Homegrown garlic is so much more flavorful than the store-bought kind, even after being stored most of the winter.

We will probably have an abundance of garlic at harvest time (usually around June), so if you want to taste-test a few cloves before growing your own, let us know! Our contact information can be found on our Request Page.

Chesnok Red

Purple-Stripe Hardneck Garlic

Chesnok Red is a great little garlic that is widely available in seed catalogs. Cloves have a beautiful purplish hue to them. This variety originated in the Republic of Georgia in extreme Eastern Europe.

Georgian Crystal

Porcelain Hardneck Garlic

Georgian Crystal is another variety from Georgia (the country, not the state). This is a large, white garlic that is said to store well, and we certainly haven't had any issues from our stored supply!

Russian Red

Purple-Stripe Hardneck Garlic

Another beautiful purple-striped variety, our supplier says this is one of their favorites. Said to be great for roasting and have a longer storage life than most purple-stripe varieties.
