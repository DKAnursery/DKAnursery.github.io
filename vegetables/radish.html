Radish

Radishes are perhaps the most quickly maturing of any vegetable we know of. Some varieties are ready for harvest in less than 30 days from sowing! There are also longer-maturing types, called winter radishes. This year, we are sticking with the quick-growing spring radishes. These are great for inter-planting between later crops such as tomatoes or cucumbers, so that the space isn't wasted in early spring. Radishes also and add a nice zest to salads, and some winter radishes store well.

  • Pink Lady Slipper
  • Spanish Black Round

Pink Lady Slipper

Spring Radish

This spring radish is described by Seed Savers Exchange as “one of the mildest radishes, with a hint of sweetness”. An interesting departure from standard red radishes, Pink Lady Slipper is, well, pink. Said to store well for several weeks, which is not usually the case with spring radishes. We'll see how Pink Lady Slipper performs for us in 2023.

China Rose

Spring/Summer Radish

China Rose is another pink radish which is somewhat of a combination spring and winter radish. It matures too slowly for a true spring radish, but it does fairly well in spring plantings. China Rose was recommended in one of our seed starting books as the author's absolute favorite winter radish. We are interested to try China Rose in both spring and fall plantings to see how it performs.
