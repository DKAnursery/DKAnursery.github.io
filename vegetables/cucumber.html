Cucumbers

Next to tomatoes, cucumbers are our favorite garden vegetable. Whether you choose to eat them fresh or make pickles, cucumbers are a delicious vegetable. We grew Cucumbers for the first time in 2022, and they were the best we've ever tasted! With so many varieties specialized for different uses, we are sure there is a cucumber for you.

Bush Champion (F1 hybrid)

Slicing/Pickling Cucumber

This cucumber is a bush-type slicer praised highly by Burpee, and after growing a half-dozen plants in our 2022 trial, we can see why! Vines take up less space but produce an abundance of cucumbers. Resistant to some diseases, Bush Champion is so good for fresh eating that we rarely have any cucumbers left over for picklesâ€¦ so good, in fact, that it is the only hybrid vegetable we think is worth growing this year! Fruit reach a size of eight to twelve inches at optimum harvest time, approximately 55 days after sowing. Somewhat tolerant of bacterial wilt.

Edmonson

Slicing/Pickling Cucumber

Edmonson is one of the handful of white cucumber varieties available, and it looked so unique we just had to try it! Seed Savers Exchange received this Kansas heirloom in the 1980's from Clarice Cooper. The variety is named for her grandfather Edmonson who grew this variety as early as 1913. This standard-vining type produces four-inch fruit which are reported to be excellent eating at any stage and rarely ever bitter (one of the main reasons we chose Edmonson out of other white varieties). SSE also claims that Edmonson is prolific and able to withstand disease and drought. That sounds too good to be true, but we won't know for sure until we grow it out this year. The only drawback we can find is a much later maturity than most cucumbers — 70 days.
