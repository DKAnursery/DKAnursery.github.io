Onions

Last but not least is the onion, a root vegetable that deserves a better reputation. We are growing onions this year particularly for storage, but they are also great used fresh as a seasoning in a number of dishes as varied as soups and tacos. Onions generally come as white, red, or yellow, and there are various sizes.

  • Dakota Tears
  • Italian Torpedo

Dakota Tears

Yellow Storage Onion

Dakota Tears is an open-pollinated, yellow storage onion bred in the 1980's and 90's at Prairie Road Organic Seed in North Dakota (hence the name). It is highly praised by multiple seed companies, and is touted to be much easier to grow from seed than most onions. Dakota Tears Onion is an Open Source Seed Initiative (OSSI) pledged variety. Basically what this means is that this variety is in the public domain and cannot be copyrighted. All growers of OSSI seeds are subject to the OSSI pledge:
"You have the freedom to use these OSSI-pledged seeds in any way you choose. In return, you pledge not to restrict others' use of these seeds or their derivatives, by patents or other means, and to include this pledge in any transfer of these seeds or their derivatives."
There are an incredible number of OSSI varieties available, and the goal is to keep vegetable breeding stock available with the ever increasing patenting of vegetable varieties. To learn more about OSSI click here or visit the OSSI website. We are looking forward to some excellent onions this year!

Italian Torpedo

Italian Torpedo is best used shortly after harvest; it does not possess the same storage qualities of Dakota Tears. According to Burpee, this is an Italian heirloom. Although it is a pinkish-red variety, it is said to have a milder taste than most red onions. Our 2022 crop of Italian Torpedo onions was unsuccessful, but that was due to our inexperience with onions and the fact that Italian Torpedo is a moderately challenging onion to grow. We have since read up on onions and gained a good deal of experience in the meantime, so we are hoping to try again with this great little torpedo-shaped variety in 2023.
