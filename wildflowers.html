Wildflowers

Wildflowers are beautiful, and many attract butterflies, honeybees, or hummingbirds! We are hoping to add wildflowers to the list of plants we grow here at the nursery in the near future. For now, we are sticking with trees and vegetables. If you are interested in wildflowers, please see our Request Page and send us an email. We will notify you as soon as wildflowers become available! Thank you for your patience as we work to gain experience raising plants.

Annuals & Biennials

Annuals are flowers that germinate, flower, set seed, and die all in one year. These flowers often self-sow prolifically and generally keep coming back year after year.

Biennials are flowers that germinate in the first year, but don't flower until the second year. Most biennials die at the end of their second season. Like annuals, biennials often self-sow prolifically and may return in the future as well.

Perennials

Perennials are flowers that continue to come back and bloom year after year. Often perennials need divided every three or four years to keep them from crowding themselves out, but generally these are hardy, carefree plants that you can depend upon for reliable blooms from year to year. Most wildflowers fall into the category of perennials, especially the prairie flowers like Purple Coneflower or the Milkweeds. We are hoping to start some perennial wildflowers in the near future.
